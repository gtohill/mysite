Problem Solving Computational thinking for a modern world

Computational thinking benefits all STEM disciplines. Each course develops your childs problem solving abilities. The core concepts of computational thinking are:

  • Algorithmic thinking - developing a set of instructions or sequence of steps to solve a problem
  • Evaluation - ensuring a solution is fit-for-purpose
  • Decomposition - breaking a problem down into its component parts
  • Abstraction - hiding detail or removing complexity without losing the important detail required to solve a problem
  • Generalization - finding a general approach to a set of problems.

An advantage that lasts a lifetime.

Computational thinking is not coding or programing. It is the first step to translating a real-world problem into a problem a computer can understand. All our courses teach our students this fundamental skill. Given the speed of computers, integrating computational thinking with any discipline offers your child a significant advantage.
